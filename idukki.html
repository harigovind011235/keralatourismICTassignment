IDUKKI

Hike the uncharted trails. Make new acquaintances among friendly locals. Find your calling in the mist-veiled highlands. Idukki, the landlocked district in eastern Kerala, is one of the most favoured tourist destinations in South India. A plethora of experiences awaits you in these unfathomed foothills of Western Ghats. Apart from the much-vaunted destinations such as Munnar and Thekkady, numerous destinations have emerged as favourites among the travel fraternity in the past couple of decades. Panchalimedu, Meesapulimala, Amappara and Vagamon are a few of the places of interest. Idukki is home to diverse endangered species including the Bengal Tiger and Nilgiri Tahr. The Periyar Tiger Reserve and the Eravikulam National Park are also located in Idukki. Often called the spice garden of Kerala, Idukki is one of the greenest regions in the state. Let the cool mountain air fill-in your senses. Discover a new aroma in the boundless spice plantations. Behold the spellbinding view of purple-blue Neelakurinji flowers covering the Nilgiri every 12 years.

MEESAPULIMALA

Considered one of the best treks in Kerala, Meesapulimala is a Shangri-la for hikers. The peak bears a resemblance to a tiger and its whisker and hence the name Meesapulimala, derived from the local language. Resting at an altitude of 2,640 m, Meesapulimala is one of the highest peaks in Kerala. If nature is your thing, you’re in for a treat. The trail is much like a Rousseau painting coming to life. Chase the wild butterflies to the abyss, stroll along with the myriad rhododendron trees, toss your worries into the isle of clouds. You can even get some local company on the way, like the Wild Gaur or the Nilgiri Tahr. Sambar Deer and Sloth Bears have also been spotted in the area.

THEKKADY

Hum along with the winged friends from distant lands. Give company to the innumerable Otters in the lake. Take your senses for a treat in any of the spice shops in the town. Explore Thekkady on your own chords. Beloved by sightseers and backpackers alike, Thekkady has something treasured in her for every traveller. Thekkady is part of the Periyar National Park, a protected Elephant and Tiger Reserve in Kerala. Named after Kerala’s longest river, the Periyar park houses many endangered species including the Bengal Tiger. Boating in the Thekkady lake is one activity that should not be missed. Tickets can be booked online (www.ktdc-boating.com) or directly from the counter. If you are looking to experience nature up close, you can always trek into the unsullied trails in the hills. Guided treks of different magnitude are available; choose one in consonance with your physical fitness. You can always go the extra mile and opt-in the Periyar Tiger Trail. Accompanied by armed guards, experience the ethereal flora and fauna of the Western Ghats. If luck favours your side, you can even spot a Bengal Tiger. A trip to Thekkady is never complete without the Spice plantation visit. Explore the variety of spice cultivations with a guided tour. You can also get handcrafted souvenirs and high-quality spices from the local market

ARUVIKKUZHI

The wind feels less friendly, but we are not offended. For she has grown weary, blowing between bohemian customs and magnificent cultures. But before this fateful reunion, a dip in the Aruvikkuzhi waterfall plunging 1000 m down makes her a little calm and serene to our wonderment. On her way up the mountain, the sandalwood trees grant her a luxurious scent that stirs one’s soul like no other. Is this what it feels like to be in heaven? Aruvikkuzhi waterfall is one of the less explored destinations in Idukki. ‘Aruvi’ in the local language translates as ‘stream’. Loosely dubbed as the stream that falls into the pit, Aruvikkuzhi waterfall can be best explored from Aruvikkuzhi Viewpoint. One of the notable features of the viewpoint IDUKKI 4 is that it is set on both sides of a stream flowing down the mountains with a bridge to separate them. To enter the viewpoint, a charge of Rs. 20 is levied for adults and Rs. 10 for children. The numerous brick walkways that zigzag along the park lead you to many scenic places with magnificent views and comfortable relaxing huts
